Maternal Mortality

An analysis of maternal mortality in the USA

Goal of the project:

Understand why the Maternal Mortality for Black women in the USA is much higher than for other races.

Technologies used:

  • Language: Python
  • Libraries: Pandas, Matplotlib, Seaborn, Numpy, Sklearn, Statsmodels

Skills:

  • Technical / analytical skills: Data cleaning & wrangling, feature engineering, exploratory data analysis, machine learning (logistic regression)
  • Soft skills: Leadership, communicating technical concepts to a non technical audience

Development of the project and challenges:

This was the first data science project that I worked on, and I had the opportunity to lead a team of fellows during the Data Science for All Fellowship. The project challenged me to use my recently acquired knowledge to do an analysis of a complex subject in a limited time, as well as to communicate technical concepts to a non technical audience. We also had to quickly pivot to a different analysis after losing 3 team members in the middle of the fellowship.

Meteorological Disasters

An analysis of meteorological disasters in the USA

Business problems:

  • Find out which storm event types are more likely to become disasters, and in which locations, as measured by the number of deaths, injuries, and economic damage they cause.
  • Conduct a preliminary assessment of whether the Post-Katrina Emergency Management Reform Act of 2006 had any impact on the severity of the disasters that occurred after the bill was signed.

Technologies used:

  • Language: Python
  • Libraries: Pandas, Matplotlib, Seaborn, Folium, Pingouin

Skills:

  • Data analysis
  • Data visualization
  • Summary Statistics
  • Hypothesis testing (t-test)

About:

This work was done as an Extended Case required for graduation in the Data Science fellowship by Correlation One.

The Extended Case was structured in a way that we had to code and analyze the data in order to answer the specific business questions.

Nuclear Energy Dashboard

A Dashboard to assist decision making and promote a deeper analysis of the company

Goals of the project:

  • Establish indicators for monitoring the performance, market conditions and trends in the nuclear energy sector.
  • Provide a tool to assist executive management decision making.
  • Offer benchmarking for industry comparison.

Skills:

  • Data analysis
  • Feature engineering
  • Business Intelligence
  • Information gathering

Development of the project and challenges:

Brazil has only 1 nuclear energy company (Eletronuclear). Therefore, establishing benchmarking involved looking at data from other countries, performing feature engineering and, for monetary data, updating and converting values to account for exchange rate fluctuations and inflation.

This project resulted in the publication of the article The Use of a control panel to evaluate a nuclear utility global performance and was presented at the 6th ELAEE/IAEE (Latin American Energy Economics Meeting).
